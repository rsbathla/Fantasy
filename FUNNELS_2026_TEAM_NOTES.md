# 2026 Defensive Funnel Notes — all 32 teams (DRAFT for review)

**What a funnel is:** a defense funnels opposing fantasy production toward its *weak* dimension.
- **PASS-FUNNEL** (tough run D + soft coverage) → target opposing **WR/TE**.
- **RUN-FUNNEL** (tough coverage + soft run D) → target opposing **RB**.
- **FORTRESS** (tough both) → fade opposing skill players.
- **SIEVE** (soft both) → smash spot, target everyone.

**Inputs & honesty caveats (read first):**
- **Funnel pctls** = our normalized 2026 engine (`defense.json`): 2025 per-player PAA-per-play **reweighted onto 2026 rosters** (movers + rookies). Higher pctl = tougher. So these already bake in roster moves — but the *rates* are 2025 quality, and **defense is low-stickiness**, so treat as priors, not locks.
- **OC estimate** = **Clay 2026 projected pass rate** (validated this session: ~2–3 pt median error vs actual on healthy teams; biggest 2025 misses were all QB injuries, e.g. ARI Murray 5 g). It's the best OC-change signal we have. **Explicit OC-hire attribution is only included where sourced (corpus); the full 2026 OC/DC list is not yet assembled** — flagged where relevant, not asserted from memory.
- **Scheme (man/zone, blitz) coordinator layer is still pending** for most teams — these notes are personnel-funnel, not yet scheme-adjusted.
- **The big forward edge** is where 2026 *adds* should shift the funnel away from the 2025-based pctl (flagged "SHIFT").

## Comprehensive OC read — additional findings (beyond Clay's pass%)

**The big one: "new OC" ≠ "new offense."** League-wide, 18 HCs call their own offensive plays; only **8 teams are "HC-as-CEO"** (LAC, WAS, PHI, ATL, NYG, DET, NE, HOU) where a coordinator runs it; and only **4 brand-new play-callers** get a first crack in 2026: **WAS David Blough, PHI Sean Mannion, BAL Declan Doyle, SEA Brian Fleury.** So a lot of the "21 new OCs" are support staff, not scheme-setters. This re-buckets the changes:

- **Continuity — ignore the OC-title churn (no scheme change; Clay already = no change):** **BUF** (Joe Brady, now HC, still calls plays), **DEN** (Sean Payton calls; Webb internal promo), **LAR** (McVay calls; Scheelhaase internal promo), **KC** (Reid calls; Bieniemy is support/reunion).
- **New HC sets the offense (real change, attribute to the HC, not the OC):** **ARI = Mike LaFleur** (Shanahan wide-zone/PA — *not* "Hackett's offense"), **ATL = Kevin Stefanski** (wide-zone + heavy PA, **TE-friendly** → Kyle Pitts upside (ATL). NOTE: Harold Fannin is a CLEVELAND TE, not Atlanta — a scheme comp I wrongly listed here), **LV = Klint Kubiak** (⚠️ correction: Kubiak is HC/play-caller, Janocko is the titular OC — Kubiak's **27.9% play-action, RB-friendly** scheme → **Jeanty + Bowers boost**), **PIT = Mike McCarthy**, **CLE = Todd Monken**.
- **OC genuinely runs it (real change), incl. first-time play-callers:** **LAC McDaniel**, **TB Zac Robinson**, **MIA Slowik**, **TEN Daboll**, **NYJ Reich**, **NYG Nagy**, **DET Petzing**, + first-timers **WAS Blough / PHI Mannion / BAL Doyle / SEA Fleury**.

**Scheme-tree clustering (Shanahan/McVay) — a consistent fantasy signature.** A large share of the *real* new offenses are Shanahan/McVay-tree: **LAC (McDaniel), TB (Z. Robinson), MIA (Slowik), ATL (Stefanski), ARI (LaFleur), LV (Kubiak)**. Common effects Clay's pass% does NOT fully capture: **quick game + heavy play-action, pre-snap motion, zone-run, and RB pass-game usage** → boosts QB efficiency, **pass-catching RBs**, and **YAC/slot WRs**; tends to *compress* raw pass rate (run-game-friendly) even as efficiency rises.

**Player-specific adds (the "additional" stuff worth flagging on the board):**
- **LAC:** McDaniel quick-game (Herbert 2.4s release) + zone run → **Omarion Hampton** RB value, efficiency up; risk = OL/sacks (timing offense needs a clean pocket).
- **LV:** Kubiak PA scheme → **Ashton Jeanty** (RB-friendly) and **Brock Bowers** (PA/TE) both rise.
- **PHI (Mannion):** scheme historically targets RBs → **Saquon expanded receiving role**, **DeVonta Smith** larger uncontested target share.
- **TB (Z. Robinson):** spread + motion → **Bucky Irving** more receptions; Mayfield bounce-back.
- **ATL (Stefanski):** 12-personnel/TE-friendly → **Kyle Pitts** is the ATL TE who benefits (confirmed ATL). [Correction: Harold Fannin is a *Cleveland* TE — cited only as a scheme-ceiling comp, NOT an Atlanta player.]
- **CHI (Ben Johnson, evolving):** most explosive-play offense in NFL, **13-personnel multi-TE** (Loveland + Kmet) → Caleb Williams + the TE room.
- **MIA (Slowik):** Shanahan scheme but **WR room gutted** → **Achane's** role is the question, not a clean boost.

**Impact on our assumptions:**
1. **Down-weight the "OC change" flag for continuity teams** (BUF, DEN, LAR, KC) — nothing actually changed; don't infer a shift.
2. **Attribute the offense to the play-caller, not the OC title** — fixes ARI (LaFleur), LV (Kubiak), CHI (Johnson), CLE (Monken), PIT (McCarthy).
3. **Widen uncertainty for the 4 first-time play-callers** (WAS, PHI, BAL, SEA) — Clay's pass% (validated ~2–3 pt) is *least* reliable for unproven play-callers; treat their projections with bigger error bars (ties into the anti-overfit branch).
4. **The Shanahan/McVay cluster** argues for a scheme tag on offense (PA-rate, RB-pass-usage) as a future feature — Clay gives volume, not these target-distribution effects.
