# 2026 Playcaller Profiles — Deep Dive

Per-playcaller detail for the teams whose **actual play-caller** changed (or whose OC title changed but caller did not). Sources: `scheme_2026.json` (web-researched June 2026, cited, re-verified) + analyst scheme research (beat writers, PFF/ESPN/SI, and a charted "Beyond the Box Score" coaching-changes breakdown). Tendency ranks are from that charting unless noted. **Provenance flag:** assignments were verified in June 2026 but not re-checked this session — live-verify any team before you cite it as fact. NE flagged separately below.

The throughline: **most of the real new offenses are Shanahan/McVay-tree** — quick game + heavy play-action, pre-snap motion, under-center, zone run, RBs in the pass game. That cluster *raises efficiency and pass-catching-RB value while compressing raw pass rate*, and it tends to *spread* target distribution (tempers WR1 target concentration). Clay's pass% prices volume, not these distribution effects.

## 🟥 New HEAD COACH calls the offense (credit the HC, not the OC title)

### ARI — Mike LaFleur (HC, new; Gannon fired). OC Hackett does NOT call plays.
- **Tree:** Shanahan/Kyle (was SF/NYJ OC). **Scheme:** wide-zone + heavy play-action, pre-snap motion, under-center looks. Among the heavier motion + PA play-callers.
- **Fantasy:** Murray gone → pocket-QB fit; efficiency should rise, but target distribution gets *spread/watered down* — temper any Cardinal WR1's target-concentration expectation even as team efficiency improves. Don't model this as "Hackett's offense."

### CLE — Todd Monken (HC, new)
- **Tree:** Air Coryell vertical. **Scheme:** vertical play-action, ~68% motion (high), RBs featured. This is the one genuinely *vertical* new offense in the group.
- **Fantasy:** downfield passing + motion = WR aDOT/ceiling friendly; RBs stay involved. Was *elite for Lamar in Baltimore* — that pedigree is why BAL replacing him (below) is a downgrade.

### LV — Klint Kubiak (HC, new; Janocko is titular OC)
- **Tree:** Shanahan/Kubiak. **Scheme:** wide-zone, heavy motion + 12-personnel, deep PA bootlegs, **27.9% play-action**, RBs as receivers. Made Alvin Kamara elite as a rookie rusher in NO — **RB-friendly** is the signature.
- **Fantasy:** **Ashton Jeanty** is the clean beneficiary (RB-friendly scheme). **Brock Bowers** gets a PA/TE boost *but soften it* — the charted breakdown is more cautious: rookie QB (Mendoza) likely means a conservative "tight-to-the-vest" Year 1, so Bowers won't be a target monster. Keep the Jeanty call, temper Bowers.

### PIT — Mike McCarthy (HC, new)
- **Tree:** West Coast. **Scheme:** timing-based West Coast, Rodgers quick game + checkdowns, suppresses scramble.
- **Fantasy:** short-area/possession passing; checkdown-heavy lifts pass-catching backs and underneath WR/TE more than vertical X receivers.

## 🟧 New OC who genuinely calls plays (real scheme change)

### LAC — Mike McDaniel (OC, new)
- **Tree:** Shanahan. **Scheme:** Miami-style ~70% motion, play-action + screens, zone run, downfield shots *off* play-action; Herbert ~2.4s release (quick game).
- **Fantasy:** **Omarion Hampton** RB value up (zone run + RB pass game), Herbert efficiency up. **Risk:** timing offense needs a clean pocket — OL/sack issues would undercut it. Target distribution spreads (temper a single WR's share).

### MIA — Bobby Slowik (OC, new)
- **Tree:** Shanahan (ex-HOU OC). **Scheme:** motion/PA/screens, horizontal spacing — *not* a vertical bomber.
- **Fantasy:** scheme is friendly, **but the WR room was gutted** — so the open question is **De'Von Achane's** role/ceiling, not a clean WR boost. Read cautiously.

### TEN — Brian Daboll (OC, new)
- **Tree:** Erhardt-Perkins (Josh Allen's developer in BUF). **Scheme:** motion + condensed splits + deep PA, WR/RB featured. New DC Gus Bradley runs heavy Cover-3 zone (separate note).
- **Fantasy:** with rookie **Cam Ward**, pass volume should **climb as Ward develops** through the year — buy the trajectory.

### NYG — Matt Nagy (OC, calls plays)
- **Tree:** Reid/West Coast. **Scheme:** West Coast/spread, misdirection, RPO, motion, for rookie-ish Dart.
- **Fantasy:** motion + pass-catching-friendly; Reid-tree QB development angle.

### NYJ — Frank Reich (OC, new)
- **Tree:** veteran (Frazier/Coryell lineage). **Scheme:** RB/TE motion to ID coverage, quick game for Geno, strict pocket (suppresses scramble).
- **Fantasy:** quick-game, possession-leaning; underneath targets and RB receiving over deep shots.

### DET — Drew Petzing (OC, new; replaced Ben Johnson's successor Morton)
- **Tree:** under-center/PA family. **Scheme:** legit **heavy under-center + play-action** (shows up strongly in the charting), motion + RPO; but his Arizona scheme skewed run-heavy/short/checkdown, Goff is a pocket QB.
- **Fantasy:** under-center PA boosts Goff + play-action receivers; watch whether the explosive Ben-Johnson juice carries over or regresses toward Petzing's more conservative Arizona baseline.

### DEN — Davis Webb (OC, new caller; Payton handed it off)
- **Tree:** Payton/spread-Air-Raid roots. **Scheme:** spread + vertical (with Waddle as a field-stretcher), deep RB room.
- **Fantasy:** vertical tilt; note this *flipped from continuity* — Payton delegating to Webb makes it a real (if Payton-influenced) change.

### CAR — Brad Idzik (OC, new caller)
- **Tree:** Canales/Shanahan-ish. **Scheme:** wide-zone committing to **more motion**, short aDOT, RB screens.
- **Fantasy:** short-area, RB-screen friendly; lower-aDOT WR usage.

### TB — Zac Robinson (OC, new; McVay branch)
- **Tree:** McVay. **Scheme:** motion/PA/wide-zone for Mayfield. Bowles DC unchanged.
- **Fantasy:** **Bucky Irving** more receptions, Mayfield bounce-back — *but target distribution gets watered down/spread*, so temper a WR1's target-concentration expectation.

### ATL — Tommy Rees (OC) under HC Kevin Stefanski's system
- **Tree:** Stefanski/Shanahan wide-zone. **Scheme:** **under-center #1 (2024), wide-zone + heavy PA**, bottom-third motion, possession passing, strict pocket.
- **Fantasy:** **TE-friendly** (12-personnel) → **Kyle Pitts** upside is the headline ATL beneficiary. (Correction from earlier draft: Harold Fannin is a *Cleveland* TE — only a scheme comp, not an Atlanta player.)

## 🟨 First-time play-callers — real change, WIDEN the error bars (Clay least reliable here)

### BAL — Declan Doyle (OC, first-time)
- **Scheme:** Payton/Johnson-tree motion + explosive PA + Lamar bootlegs (the dials are all +1).
- **⚠️ Downgrade risk:** he replaces **Todd Monken, who was elite for Lamar** — replacing an elite caller with a first-timer is a *downgrade* risk, not the reflexive "new OC = improvement." Treat BAL's offense with caution.

### SEA — Brian Fleury (OC, first-time; Kubiak left for the LV HC job)
- **Scheme:** ~80% the same wide-zone/under-center/PA family but a new voice + more pre-snap motion/tempo (Fleury from SF); Darnold is a pocket QB.
- **⚠️ Downgrade flag (important):** **Kubiak was Seattle's 2025 play-caller and the reason JSN (Jaxon Smith-Njigba) broke out** (condensed formations, under-center, route design). Handing it to an unproven Fleury makes **JSN/the SEA pass-game a downgrade *risk*, not neutral.**

### PHI — Sean Mannion (OC, first-time; Shrine Bowl OC)
- **Tree:** McVay/LaFleur (motion wide-zone — *not* run-heavy quick game). HC Sirianni is CEO; Hurts has designed runs.
- **Fantasy:** scheme historically targets **RBs** → **Saquon's receiving role** could expand; **DeVonta Smith** a larger uncontested-target share. But modeled pass attempts are *down* after A.J. Brown's exit — don't assume a WR-friendly tilt; neutral-to-cautious for a first-time caller.

### WAS — David Blough (OC, first-time)
- **Scheme:** more under-center/balanced, **less shotgun** for Jayden Daniels (dials: ↓ motion, ↓ scramble).
- **Fantasy:** the dials suggest a *more grounded, less-scramble* Daniels — a meaningful style change for a QB whose 2025 value leaned on mobility. Widest error bars of the group.
