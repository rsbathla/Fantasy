# Full Breakdown — "Beyond the Box Score": 2026 Coaching Changes pod
*Hosts: Dan Schneier + Jacob Gibbs. Guest: Rich Hribar (@LordReebs, Sharp Football). ~2h05m. Source video USMpxJMei1w → our whisper transcript. Names de-mangled.*

## Premise & method
- Thesis: **nail the new play-callers and you win your league** — last year that was Liam Coen (JAX) and Ben Johnson (CHI), who delivered Trevor Lawrence (league-winning QB), DeAndre Swift, Colston Loveland.
- They grade by **play-caller**, weighting three "cheat-code" tendencies (from Hribar's spreadsheet): **motion-at-snap rate, play-action rate, under-center dropback rate**, plus fantasy-points-produced history. They also stress **schedule** and **target concentration** (spread-out offenses are less fantasy-useful even if efficient).

## The "cheat-code" data (tendency leaders)
- **Motion at snap:** Zac Robinson #1 ('25), **Ben Johnson #2** (2 yrs), Shanahan, M. LaFleur, Monken (rising). **Nick Caley/HOU = LAST.**
- **Play-action:** McVay #1, **Ben Johnson #2, Joe Brady #3 (up from 25th)**, Kubiak, O'Connell, M. LaFleur, Petzing.
- **Under-center dropback:** **Stefanski #1 ('24)**, **Petzing #1 ('25 / #5 '24)**, Ben Johnson, O'Connell, McVay, Kubiak, McDaniel, LaFleur.
- **Fantasy-points consistency:** **Ben Johnson** = only play-caller top-10 in non-QB FP each of last 3 yrs; Shanahan + Joe Brady top-10 total 3 straight; Schottenheimer, Zach Taylor, Monken, O'Connell, McVay = top-10 in 2 of 3.

## Mega-trend they flag
The league has gone **all-in on the Shanahan/McVay tree (~18 play-callers)** + **heavy multi-TE (12/13-personnel)**. Some teams are built for it (DET, CHI, LV, BAL); others (JAX) risk hurting themselves chasing the trend off their best personnel.
