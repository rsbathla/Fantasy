## New York Jets (NYJ)

1. **Verdict.** Ceiling tier **LOW — score 22.8, rank 31/32**, essentially the floor of the league. Full-season identity: a slow-paced (13th-pctl), high-motion (64%) grind offense in a bottom-tier scoring environment (env 18, 5.5-win total) with real QB uncertainty. Draft bottom line: **fade the team as a stacking vehicle**; mine it only for the two individually great values — Garrett Wilson and Breece Hall — who carry despite the environment.

2. **Full-season environment.** Frank Reich (new OC) inherits a 53.7% pass-rate, 58.1-play offense that projects to trail often. The ceiling drivers that exist are thin: **Pass rate 9.3, Scheme upgrade 7.8, Shootout script 5.9, Env quality 5.1** — but all four are swamped by the fade flags (QB uncertainty caps ceiling; shaky OL at 18th pctl). The real limiters are structural: low implied totals and few plays. The **defense is a PASS-funnel** (soft coverage 20th pctl, stout run D, light rush) with CB1 Nahshon Wright graded "exploitable" — so opponents can throw on the Jets, which paradoxically props up *opposing* stacks more than Jets pieces. Minkah Fitzpatrick and rookie CB D'Angelo Ponds arrived but the pass-D remains a funnel.

3. **Schedule arc.** Advance phase is a **gauntlet** — nearly every Jet carries a "brutal slate of opposing defenses (7th–10th toughest)" flag, which suppresses the accumulation you need to reach the playoffs. Best early spot is **Week 4 vs CHI** (multiple players' best_wk). Playoffs: **W15 ARI, W16 NE, W17 vs MIN (game env only 23.6)** — the championship week pits a capped Jets offense against Minnesota in a low-total game. This is a tough-finisher pattern: no phase is favorable.

4. **Key fantasy players.**
   - **Garrett Wilson (WR, ADP 39.0, rank 42, −3 value).** The one must-own. 15.0 proj pts/g, 36.8 ceiling, 95th-pctl projection/advancement, 154 targets, 103 rec. Elite vs man (2.49 YPRR, 87.5 pctl; 84th man bucket) and a red-zone hog (1.2 inside-20/g, 12 end-zone). Levers: boundary X vs beatable outside CBs; RZ equity; shootout scripts. Scheme-fit best weeks are the man-heavy slates (W3 DET, W16 NE); worst is **W17 MIN** (light single-high). Cap flag: 2nd-pctl explosive rate — he's volume, not big-plays.
   - **Breece Hall (RB, ADP 31.9, rank 32).** 16.3 proj, 37.4 ceiling, 91st projection, 265 carries + 64 targets. Zone-lean receiving back (1.7 zone vs 1.11 man YPRR) who eats vs light boxes/two-high. Best week again W4 CHI; W15 ARI up-week (1.0). A genuine RB2 anchor whose ceiling is real despite the offense.
   - **Kenyon Sadiq (TE, ADP 159.2, rank 154, +4).** 9.4 proj, 79th projection, 90 targets — a *cheap* startable TE, one of the few Jets who beats his market.
   - **Adonai Mitchell (WR, ADP 177.1)** and **Omar Cooper Jr. (150.0)** are dart-throw WR3s; Mitchell is a Cover-3 killer (86th) with a boundary/vertical profile but 6th-pctl boom.

5. **Stacks & correlation.** Best stack is thin: **Geno Smith (r16, late) + Garrett Wilson (r4) + Adonai Mitchell (r15)**, stack_score just 0.4327 — the lowest-ceiling QB stack in this set. The value is that Geno is a pure last-round dart, so a skinny **Geno + Wilson** costs you nothing beyond Wilson himself. **W17 game is MIN@NYJ**; the bring-back is where the money is — **Justin Jefferson (r1), Jordan Addison (r9), T.J. Hockenson (r14)** off a superior Minnesota offense. Across the full season, correlation logic says: draft the individual Jets you love, then correlate *against* them in Week 17 via Vikings.

6. **Draft takeaway.** **Fade NYJ as a stack; roster the parts.** Wilson (r4) and Hall (r3) are strong standalone values you'd take anyway; do not reach for Geno-centered correlation in a 22.8-ceiling, 23.6-env championship game. If you own Wilson/Hall, your natural Week-17 hedge is a Vikings bring-back, not a Jets double-stack. Go-to build: use NYJ for two premium skill anchors, get your stack ceiling elsewhere.

## Philadelphia Eagles (PHI)

1. **Verdict.** Ceiling tier **HIGH — score 66.8, rank 12/32**, tied with SF at the top of the non-elite bracket. Full-season identity: a run-leaning (49.8% pass, 31st), 10.5-win contender with the softest opposing-defense slate in the field. Draft bottom line: **concentrate here for the schedule, but skinny-stack** — the run lean and "WR/TE fortress → few pass-catchers" tension caps how many Eagles you want.

2. **Full-season environment.** Sean Mannion (new OC) runs a 58.9-play, RUN-lean offense; env is only 38 despite the 10.5-win total, so ceilings come from *efficiency and scripts*, not raw volume. Ceiling drivers: **Win total 18.9, Env quality 13.0, Concentrated tree 11.0, Pace 9.6**. The team-level boom cases are lead-script-dependent (workhorse RB, early-down passing as favorites); the bust case is the classic "positive scripts kill WR volume (team milks the clock)." **Defense: strong coverage (92nd pctl), RUN-funnel** (soft run D 23rd), CB1 Quinyon Mitchell a shutdown "fortress" — meaning opponents are pushed to run, and Eagles WRs rarely see garbage-time volume.

3. **Schedule arc.** The single biggest edge is the **soft slate**: virtually every Eagle carries an "83rd–95th-pctl favorable defenses" flag — Hurts matchup pctl **91**, DeVonta 93, Barkley 84. That makes the **advance phase a green light**. Playoffs: **W15 SEA, W16 HOU, W17 at SF (game env 66.8)** — the championship is a genuine shootout environment against a fellow HIGH-ceiling team, the best W17 spot in this set. Fast-and-sustained pattern, no soft finish worry.

4. **Key fantasy players.**
   - **Saquon Barkley (RB, ADP 13.4, rank 13).** The workhorse: 16.1 proj, 35.0 ceiling, 300 carries, 43 rec, 94th-pctl market/95th value. Man-lean receiving (1.7 vs 0.84). Playoff up-weeks climb into December — **W16 HOU 1.2, W17 SF 1.0**. Efficiency-regression flag exists but role is bulletproof.
   - **DeVonta Smith (WR, ADP 28.1, rank 23, +5 value).** The model *loves* him: 90th projection, 94th SIS value, **coverage-agnostic** (2.14 zone / 1.32 man; zone bucket 92nd, three elite zone schemes — C3 91st, C2 90th, C6 86th). 128 targets. December up-weeks W15 1.1 / **W16 1.3** / W17 1.0. Only real dip: in-division ceiling weeks (9% vs 37% out).
   - **Dallas Goedert (TE, ADP 124.3, rank 124).** 10.5 proj, 87th projection, 93 targets, single-high killer (91st bucket; C3 92nd, C1 90th). Startable TE at a fair price; big YPRR regression (2.18→1.34) is the caution.
   - **Makai Lemon (WR, ADP 87.6, +5)** is a value WR3 (75th projection) but competes behind Smith. Jalen Hurts himself (below) is the QB.

5. **Stacks & correlation.** **Jalen Hurts (r7, NOT late) + DeVonta Smith (r3)** is the cornerstone skinny, stack_score 0.6342, only ~10 rounds of cost. Hurts is a top-tier man-coverage QB (7.97 Y/A vs man, 82nd) with a rushing floor (106 carries) and the soft slate behind him. Add Goedert (r11) for a standard. The **W17 SF game (env 66.8)** is the premier double-stack target — bring back **Mike Evans (r5), George Kittle (r9), Ricky Pearsall (r9)**. Because the Eagles run when ahead, the *skinny* stack correlates better than a heavy one across the advance phase — you want Hurts + one elite pass-catcher, not three mouths splitting a low-volume passing pie.

6. **Draft takeaway.** **Concentrate, but stay lean.** The soft schedule makes PHI a top advance-phase bet, and the SF championship game is elite for correlation. Build: Barkley or Smith as your anchor, Hurts + Smith skinny (add Goedert), and a Week-17 SF bring-back. Trap to avoid: over-stacking Eagles WRs 2–3 deep behind a run-first, fortress-coverage offense that suppresses secondary target volume.

## Pittsburgh Steelers (PIT)

1. **Verdict.** Ceiling tier **MID — score 54.2, rank 20/32**. Full-season identity: a slow-paced (32nd) but genuinely **pass-heavy** offense (56.2%, 5th) with a fortress O-line (91st) and an aging QB whose ceiling is capped. Draft bottom line: **a value-stack team, not a concentration target** — the pieces (Metcalf, Pittman, Warren, Dowdle, Freiermuth) are all mid-rounds, so you can build a big stack cheaply, but the ceiling ceiling is the QB.

2. **Full-season environment.** Brian Angelichio (new OC) with a 59.6-play, top-8 pass-rate attack (mid-total env 46). Ceiling drivers are balanced: **Win total 12.6, Pass rate 12.5, Env quality 12.0, Concentrated tree 12.0** (187% vacated targets — a wide-open, concentrated tree). Fade flags: pass-heavy volume, QB uncertainty (Aaron Rodgers), and weak own pass-D driving shootout scripts. **Defense (Patrick Graham, new DC): two-high split-field, press corners, NOT a heavy blitzer; RUN-funnel** (soft run D 20th, average coverage 36th), CB1 Joey Porter Jr. runs "tight." The soft run D funnels opponents to run, which is script-neutral for the passing pieces.

3. **Schedule arc.** Advance phase is average-to-tough — the skill players carry a "19th-toughest defenses" slate flag (Warren/Dowdle matchup pctl 19). Rodgers' best week is **W1 vs ATL**; Metcalf/Pittman peak **W15 vs BAL**. Playoffs: **W15 BAL, W16 CAR, W17 at TEN (game env 55.2, opp ceiling 56.2)** — a mid-total but very winnable championship spot against a soft Titans pass-D. The **W16 CAR** week is a coverage-soft spot for the whole passing game. This is a slightly-tough-start, soft-finish arc.

4. **Key fantasy players.**
   - **DK Metcalf (WR, ADP 74.5, rank 78).** 11.5 proj, 30.3 ceiling, 113 targets, 80th projection. Zone-beater (2.26 zone; C2 93rd, C3 85th) with 99th-pctl YAC and 87th explosiveness — a boundary X. **W15 BAL is his best week.** Two-high caps him and shadow risk exists.
   - **Michael Pittman Jr. (WR, ADP 95.8, rank 94, +1).** 11.6 proj, 130 targets, 80th projection, zone-lean (C2 92nd). The two WRs split a concentrated tree; Pittman's **W17 TEN fit (+0.027)** is the best playoff scheme spot on the team.
   - **Jaylen Warren (RB, ADP 77.3) & Rico Dowdle (ADP 89.8).** A true committee: Warren 11.0 proj / 71st, 95th YAC, 86th rush-eff, receiving back; Dowdle 10.8 proj / 70th, 223 carries. Nearly identical values — own the one you can pair, or both if building the stack.
   - **Pat Freiermuth (TE, ADP 198.6, +3).** 8.1 proj, 67th projection, 71st boom, C2 93rd, zone/motion weapon — a late TE with real spike weeks and the best W14 (JAX) fit. **Aaron Rodgers (QB, ADP 163.2, late)** is the pure-value stack QB.

5. **Stacks & correlation.** This is the set's best **cheap heavy stack**: **Rodgers (r14) + DK Metcalf (r7) + Pittman (r8) + Freiermuth (r17)**, stack_score 0.6538, and every non-DK piece is r8+. That's a 4-man ceiling stack for the cost of one early WR. **W17 is PIT@TEN** — a *reciprocal* correlation goldmine, because Tennessee is *also* one of your teams: bring back **Carnell Tate (r6), Wan'Dale Robinson (r9), Gunnar Helm (r16), Calvin Ridley (r16)**. Across the advance phase the concentrated tree means when Pittsburgh throws (which is often), the correlation between Rodgers and his top-2 WRs is high.

6. **Draft takeaway.** **Value-stack, don't over-pay.** PIT is the archetypal "late-QB, cheap-pieces" build — you get a 4-wide correlated stack at a discount, ideal for the advance phase, and the W17 PIT@TEN game lets you own *both* sides. The trap: expecting a top-5 ceiling out of a Rodgers-led offense (his boom rate is 13th-pctl). Go-to build: Metcalf + Pittman in rounds 7–8, back-fill Rodgers/Freiermuth late, correlate with a Titans bring-back.
