## Arizona Cardinals (ARI)

**Verdict.** Ceiling tier **HIGH — score 73.7, rank 8/32**. A pass-leaning, high-total offense (env 76) whose own porous coverage (1st-percentile) manufactures shootouts — a genuine ceiling environment despite a 4.5 win total. Concentrate on the cheap pieces (McBride, MHJ, Michael Wilson) and pair with the late QB; this is a stack-at-value team, not a fade.

**Full-season environment.** Average pace (58th pctl, 62.3 plays/g), a **top-8 pass rate of 58.9%**, new OC Nathaniel Hackett (team_ceiling tags the playcaller as a new HC staff post-Gannon) — a scheme upgrade the model prices in. The ceiling drivers are **Env quality (weight 0.249), Pass rate (0.160), Scheme upgrade (0.093), Pace (0.085)** with a shootout-script kicker (0.071). The engine of the whole thing is the defense: **soft coverage (1.6 pctl), soft run D (10.9 pctl), elite pass rush (92 pctl)**. That produces a RUN funnel and a TE funnel, but the more important best-ball fact is that a 1st-percentile secondary keeps the Cardinals' own games high-total — the "weak own pass-D → shootout volume" fade flag is a *feature* here. The offense trails often (4.5 wins), which means pass volume with the game in front of them.

**Schedule arc — full season.** McBride's best regular-season spot is **W4 @ NYG**; his scheme-fit zone weeks land **W6 & W10 vs LAR (heavy zone 79%)** against his 95th-pctl zone strength. Advance-phase target volume is stable (McBride 155 targets, 100th-pctl advancement). The playoff run: **W15 @ NYJ, W16 vs NO, W17 ARI @ LV (game env 52.2, LV opp ceiling 30.7)**. That W17 is a middling shootout — playable but the softest of the elite-tier finales. McBride's playoff-week upside ticks *up* into the final (w15/16/17 up 0.7/0.7/0.8), so this is a build that gets better, not worse, as you approach the championship.

**Key fantasy players.**
- **Trey McBride (TE) — ADP 24.2, adj_rank 22 (pos #2), model +1.** 15.0 pg / 35.9 ceiling, **99th projection, 99th advancement, 99th SIS value**. Elite volume (112 rec / 155 targets), 58% slot, 53% motion, red-zone hog (**1.6 inside-20/g, 27 end-zone**). Coverage read: **zone-beater (2.263 vs 1.483 YPRR zone/man, both seasons)** — cash him vs LAR twice. The one caution is the trend (YPRR 2.14→1.78) and a 3rd-pctl spike-week, so he's a floor-anchoring positional cheat code more than a tournament-week detonator.

Levers: **wins separation vs man (2yr)** and contested-catch equity → cash vs press/man; best spots **W4 @ NYG and W5 @ DET (heavy man)**. RZ: 0.8 i20/g, 31 end-zone.

- **Jeremiyah Love (RB, rookie) — ADP 27.3, adj_rank 28 (pos #13).** 16.5 pg / **36.8 ceiling**, 92nd projection/advancement, three-down workhorse (255 carry / 86 targets), Notre Dame ceiling pctl 97.3. **Stacks & correlation.** Best menu stack is **heavy: Brissett (R14) + McBride (R3) + MHJ (R6) + Wilson (R7)**, stack_score 0.747, value_ct 3, latest round 14 — a fully late-QB build (qb_late True). W17 **ARI @ LV** bring-backs: **Brock Bowers (ADP 20), Jalen Nailor (152.7), Tre Tucker (159.8)** — Bowers is the clean high-end bring-back to pair with a McBride final. Because the QB is free, you can build the entire pass-catcher tree at or below market and still spend early capital elsewhere (Love at 27, McBride at 24). Correlation works all season: soft own coverage → the Cardinals' games stay high-total in the advance phase, not just W17.

**Draft takeaway.** A concentrate-and-stack team where the value is structural: elite positional anchors (McBride pos #2, Love pos #13) plus mid-round WRs *and* a punt QB that all correlate. Go-to build: McBride or Love early, add MHJ (R6) and Wilson (R7), close with Brissett (R14) and a Bowers bring-back. The trap is over-drafting Brissett's ceiling — treat him as the free glue on a shootout tree.
