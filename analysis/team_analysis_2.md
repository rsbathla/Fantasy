## Dallas Cowboys (DAL)

**Verdict.** Ceiling tier **ELITE — 82.7, rank 3/32**. A fast-pace (100th-pctl), pass-leaning shootout machine whose own soft coverage (23 pctl) manufactures the back-and-forth games that spike WR/TE/QB volume. Bottom line: a **concentrate-and-stack** offense — this is one of the eight best ceiling environments in the league and its skill players are the point of the roster, not the QB.

**Full-season environment.** Up-tempo, 55.4% pass rate (11th), 65.6 plays/g, env index 94, 9.5-win total, playcaller continuity under Schottenheimer (no new-OC noise). The ceiling drivers are environment-led: env_quality 0.28, pace 0.16, win_total 0.157, pass_rate 0.115 — pure "more plays in high-total games." The defense is the fantasy accelerant: soft coverage (23), soft run D (17), light rush, tagged an explicit **PASS funnel + WR1 funnel** (CB1 Cobie Durant, 65.1 cov, "exploitable"). Opponents throw and Dallas trails-or-races — both scripts push the passing game.

**Schedule arc — full season.** The advance phase is a genuine shootout slate: fade_flags include "weak own pass-D -> shootout volume," and best-week markers cluster early (Lamb/Pickens/Ferguson all flag **Wk 1 vs NYG** as a top scheme-fit game, heavy single-high 63% meeting their single-high strengths; Dak's best_wk 5 vs TB). This is a **fast starter** you can trust to bank points before the bye (Wk 14). Playoffs: **W15 @ LAR, W16 vs JAX, W17 @ NYG** — and W17 is the payoff, game env **73.1**, NYG playing heavy single-high (63% vs 53% league) which is precisely where Lamb (92nd pctl vs single-high) and Pickens (95th) live. Note the middle-round wrinkle: JAX in W16 is a strong-coverage defense (98 pctl), the one tough playoff matchup, so W15/W17 carry the run.

**Key fantasy players.**
- **CeeDee Lamb — WR, ADP 10.4, adj_rank 10 (WR6).** Proj 17.5 pg / 42.5 ceiling (97th ceiling pctl), 158 targets. Scheme-fit is man-and-single-high elite: 97th vs man (3.66 YPRR, 99th pctl), Man Cover 2 / Cover 1 / Cover 2 / Cover 3 all 88-97. RZ hog (1.1 inside-20/g, 22 EZ). Best game **W17 vs NYG** (+0.052 fit). Advance-phase floor + championship-week peak in the same profile.
- **George Pickens — WR, ADP 24.3, adj_rank 25 (WR11).** The boom lever. Proj 14.1 / 35.7 ceiling, but 93rd-pctl Boom, 98th-pctl vs man (3.42 YPRR), contested-catch 80th pctl, and a bigger W17 edge than Lamb (+0.091 fit vs NYG). Boundary-X vertical who spikes exactly in this offense's shootouts. RZ: 1.3 inside-20/g, 31 EZ looks.
- **Dak Prescott — QB, ADP 79.6, adj_rank 77 (QB8), +6 vs ADP.** Model edge over market. 17.8 pg / 35.1 ceiling, PassGrade jumped 67.2→85.4, 94th-pctl under-pressure YPA, 8.2 Y/A vs man (man-beater). The late-ish QB that lets you spend up on the WRs.
- **Javonte Williams — RB, ADP 38.2, adj_rank 40 (RB17).** Workhorse tag, 287 carries / 59.4% carry share, Elusive 37→76. Polarizing but the volume is real in a top-5 TD offense; ceiling 35.1.
- **Jake Ferguson — TE, ADP 128.3, adj_rank 128 (TE14).** Zone-leaning volume TE (96 targets, 1.0 inside-20/g, 83rd Advancement) — a cheap correlation piece, not a ceiling driver (14th-pctl Boom, volume-only path). Note his best weeks (W6 GB, W8 ARI, heavy-zone) actually run **counter** to Lamb/Pickens' single-high peaks, and W17 vs NYG is a mild negative for him (-0.044) — a diversifying, not doubling, correlation.
- **Ryan Flournoy — WR, ADP 166.4, adj_rank 160 (WR64).** The cheap man-beater dart (89th vs man, best W1/W17 vs NYG +0.037) — a WR3 lottery ticket that shares the same single-high leverage as the studs at a round-14 price.

**Stacks & correlation.** Best stacks all run through Dak (stack_score 0.723). The skinny **Dak + Lamb** costs rounds 7+1; **Dak + Pickens** (rounds 7+3) is the higher-variance play. The heavy **Dak + Lamb + Pickens + Ferguson** (latest round 14) is a legitimate 4-man onslaught given the pass funnel. W17 game-stack: NYG env 73.1, bring-backs **Malik Nabers (ADP 37.6)**, Isaiah Likely (121.9), Malachi Fields (214.7). Because the correlation is a WR1/WR2 single-high match that repeats Wk 1 and Wk 17, the stack pays in both the advance phase and the final.

**Draft takeaway.** A ceiling team to **concentrate on**. The go-to build is Lamb (R1) as anchor, then loop back for Dak (R7) plus Pickens if he slides to R3, capped with Ferguson late — a naturally correlated, shootout-leveraged core with a live championship-week matchup. Values: Dak and Pickens both grade above market. No traps here beyond the usual RB-committee noise behind Javonte.

## Denver Broncos (DEN)

**Verdict.** Ceiling tier **MID — 60.0, rank 17/32**. Pass-leaning and up-tempo, but a **low-total environment (env 38)** puts a hard cap on the ceiling despite a 9.5-win total — this is a grind-it-out, elite-defense team, not a shootout. Bottom line: **selective, not a stack-up target** — draft the individual coverage-specialist edges (Sutton, Waddle) rather than betting the offense pops.

**Full-season environment.** OC change to Davis Webb (Payton handed off play-calling), 55.9% pass (7th), 63.4 plays/g, but env 38 / off_q 39. Ceiling drivers lean on win_total (0.157), pass_rate (0.121), pace (0.112), qb_ascend (0.110) — volume and a Nix step, **not** environment (env_quality only 0.092). **Schedule arc — full season.** The defense/run-funnel makes Denver a favorite in many games, which is good for RJ Harvey checkdowns but bad for pass-catcher ceilings (game scripts shorten). Rice/Waddle-type shootouts are the exception. Best-week signals scatter (Waddle best_wk 3 vs LAR, Sutton W12 vs PIT). Playoffs: **W15 vs LV, W16 vs BUF, W17 @ NE**, W17 env 65.0 (opp NE ceiling 70.0). W16 vs BUF is the tough middle week. W17 at NE is a heavy-man matchup (34% vs 27% league) that flips several Bronco profiles positive — **Sutton (95th vs man, +0.042), Mims (99th vs man, +0.051)** — so the championship week is where Denver's man-beaters actually align, even if the total is only mid.

**Key fantasy players.**
- **Jaylen Waddle — WR, ADP 47.4, adj_rank 49 (WR26).** Zone-beater: 90th vs zone (2.65 YPRR), Cover 3/4 elite, YPRR jumped 1.53→2.19. Proj 12.5 / 32.8 ceiling, 81st Boom. - **Courtland Sutton — WR, ADP 82.6, adj_rank 82 (WR38).** The man-coverage / RZ alpha: 95th vs man (Cover 1 96th), 1.1 inside-20/g, 29 EZ. Proj 12.1 / 31.4. Best game **W17 @ NE** (+0.042). The Bronco you most want for the final.

Dobbins (ADP 107.2)** is the early-down/goal-line complement (217 carries, 79th Boom) — a true committee.

The value is the late QB — you can build Denver correlation cheaply. W17 stack: @ NE env 65.0, bring-backs **A.J. Brown (ADP 17.7)**, Romeo Doubs (120.3), Hunter Henry (155.5). Correlation is modest because the total caps both sides.

**Draft takeaway.** Not a **concentrate** team — the env-38 cap is the number that matters. ## Detroit Lions (DET)

**Verdict.** Ceiling tier **ELITE — 84.5, rank 1/32**. The single best ceiling-season environment in the model: elite scoring (env 84, off_q 100), 10.5-win total, 94th-pctl pace, and its own defense is a **PASS funnel** that keeps opponents throwing into shootouts. Bottom line: the **premier concentrate-and-stack team of the entire pool** — you want multiple shares.

**Full-season environment.** New OC Drew Petzing (replaced Morton), 53.5% pass but 64.2 plays/g and the highest offensive-quality input in the league (off_q 100). Ceiling drivers: env_quality 0.272, **win_total 0.189 (tied-highest)**, pace 0.138, plus a concentrated-tree bonus (74% vacated targets) and OL-improve. The only flag is a shaky pass-pro grade (10 pctl) — mitigated by Goff's elite self-protection. Defense: coverage 67, run D 95, rush 89, **PASS funnel** — opponents can't run on Detroit, so they throw, and the games stay high-total in both directions.

**Schedule arc — full season.** A ceiling slate throughout — soft_slate lever hits Gibbs and others, and best-week markers concentrate around **W16 vs NYG** (heavy single-high 63%) for St. Brown, Jameson, LaPorta. Fast starter with a very early bye (Wk 6), so accumulation runs almost the entire 14-week advance phase uninterrupted. Playoffs: **W15 @ MIN, W16 vs NYG, W17 vs CHI (home)**, W17 env **81.0** (2nd-highest of my eight, opp CHI ceiling 77.5). The catch: **W15 at MIN is the roster's worst scheme week** — MIN plays light single-high (34% vs 53%), grading **negative** for St. Brown-adjacent single-high beaters (Jameson -0.053, LaPorta -0.083). So the run is bookended by a tough W15 and a golden W16 (NYG) and W17 (CHI, env 81) — a genuine tough-then-explosive finish.

**Key fantasy players.**
- **Jahmyr Gibbs — RB, ADP 1.2, adj_rank 1 (RB1, overall #1).** Proj **21.3 pg / 45.7 ceiling**, 100th Projection/Advancement, 283 carries + 68 rec, 98th Explosive. The foundational pick; matchup 85, playoff_up 0.9. Nothing else in this set approaches the weekly floor-plus-ceiling combination.

Skinny Goff+St. Brown (rounds 9+1), Goff+Jameson (9+5), Goff+LaPorta (9+8); heavy Goff+ARSB+Jameson+LaPorta (latest round 9). Because Goff is late and the pieces are early, you can run a 3-4-man Detroit stack without sacrificing your early RB/WR anchors. W17 game-stack vs CHI env 81.0, bring-backs **Colston Loveland (39.8), Luther Burden III (41.9), Rome Odunze (58.6)** — a rare case where the opponent's bring-back is genuinely high-ceiling, making the W17 double-stack elite.

**Draft takeaway.** The **maximum-concentrate** team of the group. The only honest caveat is the W15 MIN scheme dip — build the elimination run around W16/W17 strength.
