## The core diagnosis (read this before the rules)

Your conviction-leverage reads validate against actual top-1% winners at **~47%** — field-average,
not clairvoyant (2021-22: 28%; 2023-24: 45-56% — you improved to average and stayed there).
Yet 2023 lost −40% and 2024 made +117% **on essentially the same read accuracy**.

**Conclusion: your edge has never been being right more often. It is (a) WHERE you deploy
(bucket/slate selection), (b) HOW you express (distinct lineups around one core), and (c) the
PAYOFF ASYMMETRY when a max-conviction read hits (McConkey Wk18 '24, SB LIX).** Every rule below
protects one of those three things. Anything that pays you little when right, or bleeds you while
wrong, is banned — no matter how good the read feels.

## R1 — SLATE GATE: never enter a slate under 4 games
- **Receipt:** 2-3-game slates: −48% on $1,178K entered (≈ −$570K), negative in playoffs (−52%)
  AND regular season (−44%), every season. The divisional/championship $4,444 MEGAs are the
  epicenter ($75-84K entered per slate). Wild-card-size (4+ games) playoffs: **+13%** — the real
  playoff edge you remembered.
- **Why it's structural:** ≤3 games = ~12-player viable pool → ownership hyper-concentrates →
  leverage physically cannot exist → skill compresses to rake-minus-variance.
- **Metric: slate game count ≥ 4, else $0.** No exceptions — not for playoffs, not for "great spot."

## R2 — CLONE BAN: one lineup, one entry. Always.
- **Receipt:** mass-duplication (SINGLE mode) lost money in every season and every bucket:
  main −8→−82%, smaller −22→−80%, showdown −4→−56%. Career drag ≈ −$700K.
- **The permitted shortcut when time-constrained:** enter true single-entry contests (your +19%
  career, +27% recent) or skip the slate. One lineup 150× is the only construction with zero
  winning splits in your entire history.
- **Metric: uniqueRosters / totalRosters = 100%** (FL shows this per contest).

## R3 — LOSS BRAKE: after a losing slate, next slate's spend ≤ last slate's
- **Receipt:** your median spend rises +19% after a loss and falls −29% after a win — inverted
  sizing. Combined with a 79% losing-slate rate, this manufactured a 13-slate losing streak and a
  −$393K drawdown run.
- **Metric: next-slate total buy-ins ≤ prior slate's if prior slate lost.** A rule, not a mood.

## R4 — CONVICTION IS YOUR SHAPE — express it through DISTINCT lineups
- **Receipt:** slates where one core fed ≥70% of your contests ran **+62% ROI** vs **−5%** on
  diversified slates. One core, many *different* lineups = your winning signature (McConkey 100%
  exposure through 92+ distinct builds). One lineup copied = R2's graveyard.
- **Metric: core exposure up to 70-90% allowed on MAIN/SHOWDOWN; duplicate lineups = 0 (R2).**

## R5 — BUCKET BUDGETS: fund the engines, defund the leaks
- **Receipt:** MAIN +35% career ($3.04M vol) · SHOWDOWN portfolios +39% (+26/+18/+151% by season,
  never negative) · SMALLER −40% (−$618K, no positive season/mode/size except wild-card 4+ games).
- **Metric per season:** MAIN + SHOWDOWN multi-entry portfolios ≥ 90% of GPP bankroll;
  SMALLER ≤ 5% and ONLY wild-card-weekend-sized (R1); dome/novelty ≤ 2% (career −39%, fine as
  entertainment, never as strategy).

## R9 — SHOWDOWN: captain the boom, differentiate the bench
- **Receipt (global-named, 97% of CPT exposure identified; corrected after Ramneik's challenge):**

## R10 — TINY SLATES FAIL BY UNDER-DIFFERENTIATION (mechanism update to R1)
- **Receipt:** on main slates your ownership deviation matches winners exactly (you 9.4pp vs
  winners 9.7pp → +55% MULTI). On 2-3-gamers winners deviated **20.6pp** from field — you only
  11.4pp (**half the required weirdness**) → −49%. Tiny slates concentrate chalk so hard that
  winning combos are assembled from the 3-5%-owned tail you never touch.
- **R1 (don't enter <4 games) stands.** If ever violated: maximum-weirdness construction only —
  the exact opposite of the dome. Your natural style sits in the dead middle there.
